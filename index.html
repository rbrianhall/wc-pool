How the pool works — a reference for everyone in

The basics

Everyone in the pool picked 5 countries from the 48 in the 2026 World Cup, before the tournament kicked off. Your picks are locked — no trades, no swaps. You earn points as your teams advance and win knockout matches, and the highest total when the dust settles after the July 19 final takes the pot.

The twist: country multiples

Every country carries a multiple set from its pre-tournament championship odds. Favorites are low (Spain ×1.1, Argentina ×1); long shots are high (Ghana ×90, Qatar ×150). Every point a team earns is multiplied by its number.

So a favorite that goes deep scores modestly, while a dark horse that overperforms can be worth a fortune. Picking is a balance between safe points and high-multiple upside.

Scoring rubric

Base points below, then × the country multiple. Knockout points require winning the match, not just appearing in it.

Example: you own Ghana ×90. Ghana advances from its group (3 × 90 = 270) and wins its Round-of-32 match (10 × 90 = 900) — that's 1,170 points from one team. A team that went all the way at ×90 would bank the full 198 base × 90 = 17,820.

How a team advances

48 teams, 12 groups of four. The top two of every group, plus the eight best third-placed teams, reach the 32-team knockout bracket. From the Round of 32 on it's single elimination — lose and you're out (except a semi-final loser, who drops into the third-place match).

Reading the leaderboard

  • Points — what you've actually banked so far. This is what decides ranks and the payout.
  • Proj — a projection of your final score: current betting markets simulated over the rest of the bracket. A live forecast, not money in the bank. Tap the headers to sort by it.
  • Best case — the most you could still finish with if every one of your live teams ran the table.
  • Behind — how far back of first place you are, on banked points.
